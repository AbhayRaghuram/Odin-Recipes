Homemade Iced Coffee


Homemade Ice Coffee is exactly as the name suggests. It it coffee thats brewed overnight in cold water and then strained and poured over ice cubes.

Recipe Ingredients:

    For The Iced Coffee:

  • 8 Ounces dark roast coffee grounds
  • 4 Quarts filtered water or tap water

    • For The Sweetened Homemade Creamer:

  • 1/2 Cup heavy cream or half and half
  • 1/2 Cup sweetened condensed milk

    • Optional Additions:

  • Coffee Syrups
  • Sugar
  • Caramel
  • Chocolate syrup
  • Cinnamon

Preparation Instructions:

Prep Time: 30 Mins, Cook Time: 8 Hours 30 Minutes, Serves: 4

  1. Pour half the water in two half-gallon pitchers. Sprinkle 4 ounces ground coffee over the water in both pitchers. Cover and let sit 30 minutes; after 30 minutes, stir.
  2. Cover again and let sit at room temperature overnight.
  3. Line a fine mesh strainer with a double layer of fine cheesecloth or a coffee filter set over a large bowl or pitcher. Pour the coffee mixture through the strainer and allow the liquid to run through. The coffee mixture will take several minutes to strain, and will need to be done about 2 cups at a time. When finished, discard the coffee grounds.
  4. Pour the strained coffee in a covered container in the fridge until cooled.

    5. For The Homemade Sweetened Creamer:

  5. In a pitcher or bowl whisk together the heavy cream and sweetened condensed milk.
  6. Store in the fridge in an airtight container until ready for use.

    7. To Assemble Your Iced Coffee:

  7. Fill a glass with ice (or better yet, coffee ice!).
  8. Pour 3/4 full with coffee.
  9. Fill the remaining 1/4 with the creamer mixture. If you prefer a sweeter and creamier iced coffee you can do equal parts instead.
  10. Add in optional add-ins if desired.

    11. Enjoy!
